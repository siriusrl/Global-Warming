How Global Warming Occurs

Burning fossil fuels, cutting down forests and farming livestock are increasingly influencing the climate and the earth’s temperature. This adds enormous amounts of greenhouse gases to those naturally occurring in the atmosphere, increasing the greenhouse effect and global warming. Many of these greenhouse gases occur naturally, but human activities are increasing the concentrations of some of them in the atmosphere.

Source: The Europian Commision


effects of global warming

Each year scientists learn more about the consequences of global warming, and each year we also gain new evidence of its devastating impact on people and the planet. As the heat waves, droughts, and floods associated with climate change become more frequent and more intense, communities suffer and death tolls rise. If we’re unable to reduce our emissions, scientists believe that climate change could lead to the deaths of more than 250,000 people around the globe every year and force 100 million people into poverty by 2030.

Source: Natural Resources Defense Council

How we can save our future eras

To save future generations from global warming, we must transition to renewable energy sources like solar and wind, drastically reduce our carbon emissions by embracing sustainable practices in transportation and industry, and protect and restore natural ecosystems such as forests and wetlands. Implementing stricter environmental regulations and investing in green technologies can drive innovation and reduce our ecological footprint. Additionally, raising awareness and educating people about climate change's impacts can foster a culture of sustainability and collective action. By working together globally, we can create a resilient and healthy planet for future generations.
